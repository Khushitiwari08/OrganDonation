Donation
Organ donation is the process of donating organs or tissues from a deceased or living donor for transplantation into a recipient who needs them. Organ donation can save lives and improve the quality of life for those who receive transplants. Deceased organ donation typically occurs when a person has died and their organs are donated for transplantation. Organs that can be donated after death include the heart, lungs, liver, kidneys, pancreas, and intestines. Tissues that can be donated include corneas, skin, bone, and heart valves.
